## Design Context

### Users

Adults across all life stages — from young adults starting to get organized, to middle-aged parents thinking about their kids and family, to older people facing end-of-life realities. The common thread is someone who cares about being prepared and leaving clarity for the people they love.

They arrive **proactive and calm** — not panicked, not grieving. They're choosing to do this. The app is used in **short, focused bursts**: open, add a few things, close. Not long sit-down sessions.

### Brand Personality

**Calm. Human. Clear.**

These three are non-negotiable. Every design decision filters through them:
- **Calm** — generous whitespace, muted palette, unhurried pacing. Nothing competes for attention.
- **Human** — warm typography (serif headings), realistic language, no clinical or legal tone.
- **Clear** — obvious hierarchy, simple navigation, no ambiguity about what to do next.

### Aesthetic Direction

- **Visual tone:** Quiet, warm minimalism. Inspired by Apple and Headspace — breathing layouts, soft surfaces, restrained color.
- **Theme:** Light mode. Warm off-white backgrounds (`#F9F8F8`), white card surfaces, sage green (`#8a9785`) as the singular accent. Dark mode is a future consideration but not current priority.
- **Palette:** Sage green primary with four pillar colors (sage, lavender, soft blue, warm blush). Neutrals are warm-tinted, never cool gray. The palette is established and intentional — preserve it.
- **Typography:** DM Sans (body) + Libre Baskerville (serif headings) are the brand fonts. These are locked in. Libre Baskerville adds warmth to headings without feeling stuffy; DM Sans is clean and readable for everything else.
- **References:** The existing app design is the reference. Build on what's there.
- **Anti-references:** Never cluttered. Never flashy. Never "in your face." No urgency, no gamification, no dense dashboards, no notification badges screaming for attention.

### Design Principles

1. **Breathing room over density** — When in doubt, add more space. Short bursts mean users scan, not study. Give every element room to land.
2. **One thing at a time** — Each screen has one job. Progressive disclosure over information overload. Don't front-load complexity.
3. **Warmth through restraint** — The calm feeling comes from what's *absent*: no competing colors, no busy borders, no stacked CTAs. Warmth lives in the serif headings, the sage green, and the generous margins.
4. **Earned trust, not demanded** — The subject matter is sensitive. The interface earns trust by being predictable, transparent, and never pushy. No dark patterns, no urgency tactics, no "complete your profile" pressure.
5. **Subtle feedback, not spectacle** — Animations are functional (press feedback, focus states, staggered list reveals). Nothing bounces, glows, or draws attention to itself. Motion serves clarity.
